Discover Dover

Rich History Since 1636

Originally settled in 1636 as part of Dedham, Dover was incorporated as an independent town in 1836. With nearly 400 years of history, Dover has evolved from an agricultural community into one of Massachusetts' most prestigious residential towns while maintaining its rural New England charm and character.

Exceptional Quality of Life

Dover is renowned for its exceptional school system, expansive properties along the Charles River, and strong sense of community. The town's commitment to preserving its natural beauty and historic character makes it one of the most sought-after addresses in Greater Boston.

Community Highlights

  • Population: 5,923 (2020 Census)
  • Located 15 miles southwest of Boston
  • Highly educated community (87% college graduates)
  • Top-rated public schools
  • Extensive conservation land and trails
  • Historic town center and meeting house
  • Active community organizations
  • Close to Legacy Place shopping
